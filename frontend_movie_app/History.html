Let's Dive Deep into the History of Movies



History of Movies

  • 1888 - The First Film

    The first motion picture, "Roundhay Garden Scene," was directed by Louis Le Prince.

    October 14, 1888

  • 1895 - The Lumière Brothers

    Auguste and Louis Lumière held the first public screening of films in Paris.

    December 28, 1895

  • 1903 - The Great Train Robbery

    Edwin S. Porter's "The Great Train Robbery" was released, becoming the first narrative film.

    December 1, 1903

  • 1927 - The Jazz Singer

    The first feature-length motion picture with synchronized dialogue, "The Jazz Singer," premiered.

    October 6, 1927

  • 1939 - The Golden Age of Hollywood

    Hollywood's Golden Age peaked with classic films like "Gone with the Wind" and "The Wizard of Oz."

    1939

  • 1995 - Digital Revolution

    The digital revolution began to transform the film industry, with movies like "Toy Story."

    November 22, 1995
